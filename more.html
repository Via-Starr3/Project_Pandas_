More about red pandas

Who is red pandas?

Red pandas, a.k.a. the lesser panda or cat bear, a mammalian predator that mainly feeds on plants. Body length is approximately 51-64 cm and tail length is 28-48 cm. Males weigh about 3.7-6.2 kg, females 4.2-6.0 kg. The body is elongated, the tail is fluffy, and the head is broad, with a short, sharp muzzle and small rounded ears. It has 38 teeth. Its paws are short, sturdy, with semi-retractable claws that help the panda to easily climb up and down trees.

Where are they living?

The home of red pandas is western China and the eastern Himalayas. At the moment, most of them live in captivity, under human supervision. In this way, the panda's life is kept safe.

Red pandas in the wildlife

In the wild, red pandas tend to stay in groups. In the wild, red pandas tend to stay in groups. They mark their territories by scent. As food, red pandas prefer plant food, such as bamboo. Interesting fact: these animals eat almost like humans; while eating, they sit down and help themselves with their front paws.

Why are they extinting?

Most red pandas die because their home is being destroyed. Recently, deforestation has not stopped, but has only gained momentum. The pandas cannot be rescued in time and are dying. Also, some of them die because of poaching. People like the beautiful red fur. Now red pandas are in the group of endangered species (EN).
